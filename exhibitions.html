Our vast wealth of research resources come to life at the state-of-the-art gallery space in the Kislak Center. Throughout the year, the gallery hosts exhibitions that highlight our distinctive collections, showcase the works of UM students and faculty, and serve as companion pieces for our events.

  • Chronicles and Cosmographies: Exploring the Earth, Air, and Seas of the Americas, featuring items from Special Collections and the Jay I. Kislak Collection of the Early Americas, Exploration and Navigation
    Opening March 20, 2018

  • Pan American University: The Original Spirit of the U Lives On
    This exhibition will feature archival materials from UM's Cuban Heritage Collection, Special Collections, and University Archives documenting the rich and multicultural history of the University. Experience a visual journey through 90 years at the U, from George Merrick's Pan Am-inspired vision and early plans to the globally oriented research institution President Julio Frenk leads today. Original photographs, letters, and publications will highlight a tradition of vision and perseverance with an emphasis on the University's enduring connection to Latin America and the Caribbean.
    Opening March 2018
